Work History

University of Kansas Medical Center: October 2023 – Current

Grant Administrator

At KUMC I worked on the pre-award team for our central offices providing online training and support for principal investigators without any department level support. I helped them build realistic budgets, meet sponsor requirements for application materials, and submitted the required information for approval and signature. I also assisted with the development of training materials that were used across campus. Specifically, I helped create job aids for the newly adopted Huron Grants software for use across campus. This position was a great way to build on my previous research administration experience and blend my business and accounting knowledge with the training strategies and curriculum development skills I’m learning in my MLIS.

University of Kansas: June 2019 – October 2023

Grant Coordinator

On the main University of Kansas campus, I maintained the department level accounting records for all awards within my assigned portfolio. I also prepared funding scenarios and conducted expenditure forecasting for award management. I met with principal investigators regularly to review award a discuss payroll to ensure payroll deadlines were consistently met. Working as a Grant Coordinator at KU allowed me to use my accounting knowledge in a university setting with a focus on research administration. This position was an import steppingstone in for my long term career goals.

Mid-Continent Public Library: September 2013 – June 2021

Technology Specialist

I worked at MCPL for almost 8 years. In my last role I had the opportunity to be an in-person technology trainer. I assisted library customers with technology-related troubleshooting. I also answered questions and provided one-to-one technology training. I would still assist with other library responsibilities as needed. This position was my first opportunity to be in a role that focused substantially on training. On reflection this position gives me a different perspective than my most recent training role since this is in person training instead of online training.
